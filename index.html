FTX Bankruptcy

January 5, 2023 by 0xGrind

This week saw the dramatic collapse of FTX, which went from a $32B empire to declaring chapter 11 bankruptcy.

Binance was established in 2017 by cz binance, and it swiftly became the biggest centralised cryptocurrency exchange in the world.

Numerous exchanges (Coinbase, BitMEX, Gemini, etc.) have attempted in vain to unseat it since its establishment.

A new participant (FTX Founded by SBF )entered the market in May 2019 and became third-largest cryptocurrency exchange globally.



Background........


Sam Bankman-Fried, the billionaire founder of FTX, launched his first company, Alameda, a hugely profitable cryptocurrency hedge fund, after beginning his career as an arbitrager of the Japanese Bitcoin premium:
After the arbitrage chances dried up, Alameda switched to become a market maker and made enormous profits using quantitative trading techniques.



Fear,Uncertainty and Doubt........


In fact, FTX was founded *after* Alameda as a platform "for traders, by traders." It gained a reputation for offering exceptional returns as well as a less favourable reputation for covertly dumping on retail.


			
And since it began, there has been a lot of wild speculation about how the two entities are related.
It doesn't help that Alameda was FTX's initial market maker, providing the exchange with the liquidity it needed to launch.

			


How it began........



			
This relationship's story has been violated, which is the root of the current issue.
The story that had been told most often about FTX-Alameda was one of blatant corruption:

Alameda receives priority orderflow from FTX, enabling its sibling hedge fund to outperform other traders.

A never-ending money maker would be taking a few privileged basis points on the third-largest exchange.

But Alameda's data source isn't FTX.

Alameda's piggy bank is FTX.

Sam sources his capital for his wildly successful trading operation from a highly profitable exchange.
Sam describes the early years of Alameda to David Rubenstein in a moment of foreshadowing:

"We were putting credit agreements together piecemeal."

Having a winning strategy but no resources to put it into action must be incredibly frustrating.

To deal with that problem, FTX was developed..
